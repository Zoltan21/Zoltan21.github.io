Tradition in Motion

Introduction

Our project's goal is to introduce local handicraft traditions and thereby strengthen community cohesion. We aim to create an inclusive environment where anyone can participate and learn. The program helps participants acquire new skills, which can assist in career choices. In addition to preserving traditions, we consider active participation important, so it’s not only about individual development, but also about strengthening our community. We emphasize:

  • the effective promotion of traditional crafts among younger audiences
  • cultural education and the organization of events
  • the development of artistic and design skills
  • the learning of traditional craft techniques
  • the use of modern communication tools in the service of tradition preservation
Why was this project created?
To revive Transylvanian craft traditions and give young people the opportunity to actively take part in carrying them forward.
Which traditions do you aim to highlight in particular?
Among others: wood carving, weaving, felting, furniture painting, herbal knowledge, and enamel art.

July – Sustainable and Eco-Friendly Forestry

In July, we held our workshop on sustainable and eco-friendly forestry. The rich forest along the Máréfalva stream provided an ideal location. We would like to express our gratitude to Uncle Laci, who contributed to the day’s success with his expertise and experience. We gained a wealth of exciting and useful information – for example, we learned that during forest planting, we should primarily think of our grandchildren, as the trees we plant today will bring value to their generation. We also learned that pest control is crucial: without proper protection, decades of work can be lost in just a few weeks.

June – Enamel Art Workshop at the Cultural House in Székelyudvarhely

In June, we held our enamel workshop, where everyone could unleash their creativity. We worked with small copper plates, which we first coated and then fired in a kiln. Next came the decoration phase: everyone painted their favorite motifs on the plates, which were then fired again. The process was not only exciting but also visually stunning – for instance, the bright orange enamel turned into a deep, elegant red after firing, which surprised us all. The shared creative experience was truly memorable and something we will surely talk about for a long time!

May – Székely Gate Carving

In May, we organized our gate carving workshop, where we explored various traditional techniques. During the session, we learned that the most widespread method in the area is the so-called "driving chisel" technique. The design is first drawn on the wood, then carved precisely along the lines using a driving chisel and hammer. The next step is to use the clearing chisel to remove excess wood around the pattern, thus highlighting the motifs. Interestingly, we found out that the driving chisel is usually made of iron and requires a hammer, while the clearing chisel is mostly wooden, suitable for finer work and used with a mallet – a smaller wooden hammer.
